Here is a step by step demonstration of the usage of Appearo:

Background

This is Elmer Wright. Elmer needs $5,000 dollars to move after finishing college. He does not have enough cash on hand so he is trying to get a loan from the local bank down the street. However, due to his thin credit history, Elmer received a quote with unreasonable interest rate. After doing some research on the Peer-to-Peer lending providers, he turns to Appearo to gain access to capital.

Machine Learning

Elmer will use Appearo’s current financial-based machine learning model to predict his creditworthiness. On the model page, he will input his credit utilization (0.1), age (23), number of dependents (0), personal debt ratio (0.5), monthly income (200), number of real estate loans (0), number of open credit lines (2), number of times 30-59 days late on a payment (0), number of times 60-89 days late on a payment (0), and number of times 90 days or more late on a payment (0). The machine learning model then outputs a 75.29% likelihood of the loan being paid back. This data would then be put on the lending platform profile page for an investor to see.

Alternative Factors

With an extensive work history and a recently obtained college degree, Appearo allows Elmer to quantify these alternative factors and use them in support of his profile. With graduation behind him, Elmer is able to mark in our platform that he has fully completed both his high school degree and his Bachelor's degree. Using their multiplicative weights (1 for high school, 3 for bachelors), Elmer gets an educational score of 4. He graduated with a GPA of 3.65 so this is also added to his profile. Elmer indicates that he worked part time (20 hrs per week) every semester of college, while taking the summer off. Following his 18th birthday and based on a 40 hour week, he has 6,691 available working hours. With his 20 hr / week job in school, he has a total of about 2,559 hours actually worked. This ratio gives a work consistency score of 0.382. Following graduation, he has an official, signed job offer letter, which he input into the system that assigns him a 1. As he just finished school and does not have any current intentions of pursuing any higher degree, he marks the college acceptance letter as no and the methodology assigns him a 0. This data can be viewed by an investor and will, eventually, be used to train an alternative factors predictive model.

Blockchain Network Investment

Elmer signs up on Appearo’s Blockchain Network. His information can be viewed by all potential investors. When investors go onto the commercialized network, they will be able to see Elmer’s creditworthiness prediction (75.29%), his alternative factors, and the log of past investments to Elmer from other investors. Based on this abundance of information, an investor can make an informed decision to invest. Once a investor’s transaction goes through on the Blockchain backend, they will see a block added with the transaction information.
