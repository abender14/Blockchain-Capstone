Alternative Factors Methodology

Why Alternative Factors?

Forty-five million people in the United States are considered ‘credit invisible’ due to having credit histories that are too thin to generate a credit score [1].

To help increase equity and accessibility in the lending process, Appearo has created a methodology to quantify non-financial factors to assess the creditworthiness of the borrower. Such factors grant borrowers more opportunity to access resources, despite being invisible to most lending institutions.

Financial Factor Assessment

A financial factors assessment can be described as a creditworthiness assessment that takes into account only attributes relating to information that is included on a credit report.

For example, credit utilization, monthly income, number of late payment, number of open credit lines, number of real estate loans, etc.

Alternative Factors Assessment

An alternative factors assessment accounts for any information not included in a credit report.

This could include financial information such as rent payments, cable payments, etc. or non-financial information such as education history, career consistency, etc.

Alternative Factor Input Type How We Quantify
Education History Scaled Numeric Value Scaled based on completed degree level (High School Diploma/GED, Associate, Bachelor, Master, Doctorate)
Additive value for education level in progress based on years completed or credits completed.
Career Consistency Scaled Numeric Value Ratio of active working months to available working months.
Each job is scaled to the number of hours worked per week.
Work availability is based on a 40 hours work week for the period after one’s 18th birthday.
All work history is included (including work prior to 18th birthday).
GPA Scaled Numeric Value Unweighted GPA (0.0-4.0)
Job Offer Letter Binary If one has a job offer then they have not started yet.
College Acceptance Letter Binary If one has a college acceptance letter for a program they have not started yet.

[1] K. P. Breevoort, P. Grimm and M. Kambara, "Data Point: Credit Invisibles," The CFPB Office of Research, May 2015. [Online]. Available: https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/201505_cfpb_data-point-credit-invisibles.pdf.
