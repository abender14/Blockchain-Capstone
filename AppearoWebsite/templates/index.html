Mission

In the current financial climate, credit is a key to gaining access to important services such as loans. Yet, forty-five million people in the United States alone do not have enough credit history to be assessed by the current system [1]. Creditworthiness is currently determined almost entirely by historical financial information, which perpetuates a cycle of inaccessibility. Additionally, sensitive financial information such as account information and social security numbers is housed in central credit agencies and banks; this creates a vulnerability to hacking.

The advent of peer-to-peer lending models, blockchain platforms, and machine learning algorithms for creditworthiness assessment presents a unique opportunity to address the problems of credit lending. Appearo works beyond the conventional creditworthiness assessment and technical constraints in order to increase security, equity, and transparency in peer-to-peer lending.

[1] K. P. Breevoort, P. Grimm and M. Kambara, "Data Point: Credit Invisibles," The CFPB Office of Research, May 2015. [Online]. Available: https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/201505_cfpb_data-point-credit-invisibles.pdf.

A New Perspective

Appearo's overarching objective is to propose a new methodology for a holistic creditworthiness assessment and create a technical solution to access and engage in peer-to-peer lending on a blockchain platform.
